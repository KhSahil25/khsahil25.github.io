In nearly every slant, Internal Communication plays a very important role to success. Communication is more than just talking face-to-face, although this form of communication should never be neglected. However, every aspect may dictate different types of communication. Communication methods such as email, telephone, messaging, in-person communication can all serve a valuable purpose and come along with a variety of benefits.

One of the biggest obstacles that employees face is how they can communicate with their seniors. They may not feel comfortable approaching the boss, and as a result of this apprehension, many employees may choose to avoid bringing up important points, thus blocking the flow of communication. The quality of internal communication often says a lot about the company itself. No matter how you cut it, some people simply tend to hesitate, even if they're adept at what they do. Identifying and reaching out to shy employees can help break down the barriers of workplace communication they may have imposed on themselves, especially if you show a genuine interest in them.

What's interesting about internal communication is that many people agree that it's wrecked and intuitively feel that it should be enhanced. Most people can admit they can work on how they communicate with others and taking inventory of how you personally approach communication can be remarkably beneficial.

Fortunately, there are plenty of ways by which you can improve workplace communication.

  • Always encourage two-way street communication. The employees will be provided with such work environment which is free from any politics and should be independent. They should be able to communicate with each other in an open communication platform.
  • Be a good listener and understand what exactly another person want to convey, if you put in the work, however, you're bound to see positive results.
  • With internal communications, think less is more. Keep things simple, brief and to the point. Focus on sending the right information to the right people at the right time. Stay focused and goal oriented.Don't deviate from the common objective.
  • Be Transparent, transparency of goals drives performance because it creates both a sense of urgency and commitment. Be sure to provide channels for your team to give feedback and share ideas, whether its regarding the workplace, potential future or the company as a whole.

Take advantage of every opportunity to practice your communication skills so that when important occasions arise, you will have the gift, the style, the sharpness, the clarity, and the emotions to affect other people. And always remember - Write To Be Understood, Speak To Be Heard, Read To Grow.
